Editorial Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Our Review Process

Every book that appears on Bithues Reading Lab has been read in full by one of our experienced reviewers. We do not publish reviews based on publisher summaries, press releases, or partial reads. Our selection process starts with reader interest — we look for books that address real questions people have, challenge assumptions, or deliver genuine insight — and applies a quality threshold to ensure only work we consider worthwhile makes it onto the site.

Books that clear those two gates move to our reading queue. We complete every title before rendering a verdict. A review that isn't finished isn't published.

How We Review

Our reviews are written by experienced readers with background in analysis, writing, or the subject matter of the book in question. Every review goes through an internal review for accuracy before publication. We check facts about the book, its author, and its publisher against authoritative sources before going live. If a review contains an error, we correct it.

Review Scoring

We don't use star ratings. Stars are inconsistent — a five-star book in one genre isn't comparable to a five-star book in another, and star inflation varies wildly across platforms. Instead, every Bithues review concludes with three clear signals:

  • Verdict — our overall recommendation, stated plainly
  • Who Would Enjoy This — the reader the book is best suited for
  • Key Takeaways — the one or two ideas worth carrying away from the book

Update Policy

Reviews are updated when a significant new edition is released — for example, a substantially revised or expanded edition that changes the book's substance. If a book receives widespread criticism for factual errors after our review is published, we investigate and update our assessment accordingly.

Correction Policy

We correct errors promptly and transparently. When a factual mistake is identified, we revise the review and add a visible erratum note at the top of the page noting the correction and the date it was made. We do not silently edit content in ways that would mislead a reader who encountered the original.

Source Standards

We cite book titles, authors, and publishers accurately, linking to Amazon for purchase convenience. We link to Amazon because it is the most widely available retailer — we want readers to be able to buy the books we recommend without friction. These are editorial links, not paid placements.

Conflict of Interest

Bithues Reading Lab has no financial relationship — no sponsorships, no paid reviews, no gift arrangements — with any author or publisher whose work appears on this site. We do not accept payment for reviews. Authors may submit books for consideration, but inclusion on the site is based solely on whether our reviewers find the work worthy of coverage. We call this no pay-to-play, and we mean it.

Privacy

We take reader privacy seriously. For information about what data we collect and how we use it, please read our Privacy Policy.
