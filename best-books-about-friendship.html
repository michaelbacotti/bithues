Bithues Reading Lab — Friendship

Best Books About Friendship — Stories of Loyalty and Connection

Last updated: April 2026

Home › Books › Friendship

Friendship is one of the least examined relationships in literature — we get thousands of books about romance and family, comparatively few that take friendship seriously as a force that shapes who we become. These books do. They examine what it costs to show up for another person, what we owe our closest friends, and what happens when loyalty is tested against self-interest.

A Man Called Ove by Fredrik Backman

Ove is a grumpy, rigidly principled widower who has decided to end his life — until a young family moves in next door and accidentally makes him care about things again. The friendship that develops between Ove and the Iranian refugee Parvaneh is the kind of unlikely connection that fiction does better than any other form. Backman writes sentiment without tipping into sentimentality, which is a rare skill.

Buy A Man Called Ove on Amazon →

The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien

The friendship between Frodo and Sam is one of the most enduring portraits of loyalty in all of literature. Sam carries Frodo up the mountain when he can't carry himself, and the book never makes a show of this — it simply shows two people bound by something that transcends circumstance. Tolkien understood that the deepest friendships aren't dramatic; they're just persistent.

Buy The Lord of the Rings on Amazon →

Endurance by Alfred Lansing

Shackleton's expedition to Antarctica in 1914 is one of the most remarkable stories of leadership and friendship in the real world. When his ship was crushed by ice, he kept all twenty-seven men alive for two years in the most hostile environment on earth — not through heroic speeches but through consistent, pragmatic care for each person. A case study in what friendship looks like under absolute pressure.

Buy Endurance on Amazon →

The Boys in the Boat by Daniel James Brown

The 1936 University of Washington rowing team — nine young men from the Depression era who won Olympic gold — is really a story about how friendship becomes the thing that holds you together when everything else is uncertain. The relationships between the rowers and their coach are specific, well-researched, and deeply human. This is sports writing at its best.

Buy The Boys in the Boat on Amazon →

The Secret Life of Bees by Sue Monk Kidd

At its heart, this novel is about the friendships that save you when your family fails you. The narrator, a fourteen-year-old girl escaping an abusive father, finds community with a group of Black women beekeepers in 1960s South Carolina. The relationships are warm, specific, and built through shared work rather than shared ideology.

Buy The Secret Life of Bees on Amazon →

Fried Green Tomatoes at the Whistle Stop Cafe by Fannie Flagg

Two timelines — 1985 and 1930s Alabama — converge around the friendship between two women who run a cafe in a small Alabama town. The story examines how friendship changes over decades, what women owe each other across racial lines in the Jim Crow South, and how memories of friendship sustain us when we're old. Flagg builds relationships that feel lived-in rather than constructed.

Buy Fried Green Tomatoes at the Whistle Stop Cafe on Amazon →

A Little Life by Hanya Yanagihara

Four college friends move to New York and the novel follows them for twenty years. The friendship between Jude, Willem, JB, and Malcolm is the book's spine — it's the relationship that keeps showing up even as everything else falls apart. Yanagihara writes about male friendship with an unusual combination of tenderness and honesty about how men fail each other. Difficult but unforgettable.

Buy A Little Life on Amazon →

The Time of My Life by Donald Hall

Hall's memoir of his friendship with the poet Robert Frost — spanning decades and two very different lives — is a precise, unsentimental account of what it means to be metabolized into another person's life. Frost was demanding, manipulative, and brilliant, and Hall had to figure out how to hold all of that in one relationship. For anyone who has had a friend who was also difficult.

Buy The Time of My Life on Amazon →

The Ocean at the End of the Lane by Neil Gaiman

A middle-aged man returns to his childhood home and remembers the friendship between a young boy and a girl who lived on a farm at the end of the lane — a friendship that was simultaneously mundane and cosmically important. Gaiman's fable is about the friendships we form before we know enough to be strategic about them, and what happens to those connections when we become adults.

Buy The Ocean at the End of the Lane on Amazon →

The Laramie Project by Moises Kaufman

A theater troupe interviewed over two hundred people in Laramie, Wyoming after the murder of Matthew Shepard, and constructed a play from those interviews. The friendships in the play — between Shepard and his community, between the townspeople who were changed by what happened and those who weren't — make it one of the most honest documents we have about what community means when you're targeted for who you are.

Buy The Laramie Project on Amazon →

💡 Key Takeaway

If you want fiction, start with A Man Called Ove or The Ocean at the End of the Lane — both are short, beautifully written, and show friendship without explaining it. If you want nonfiction, Endurance and The Boys in the Boat demonstrate friendship under pressure in ways that will stay with you. A Little Life is the deep cut — long, intense, and worth the commitment.
