The Question

1,800 words · 6 min read

Who tells your story?

I don't mean the facts — those are easy. I was born. I lived. I died. But who decides what those facts mean? Who shapes the narrative? Who chooses what to include and what to leave out?

My name doesn't matter. I've been called many things by many people. Hero. Villain. Fool. Sage. The truth is, I'm none of those things. I'm just someone who lived a life and let others tell the story.

***

There was a writer once — she wanted to tell my story. She came to me with questions, a notebook, a hungry look in her eyes.

"What was it like?" she asked. "Being there? Being part of something so important?"

I told her the truth: "I don't know. I was too busy living it to notice."

She didn't understand. How could she? She'd spent her whole life on the outside, looking in. She thought the story was the important part.

She was wrong.

***

Here's what I've learned: the story is never about what happened. It's about who tells it, and why, and for whom.

The same events, told by different people, become completely different stories. The hero becomes the villain. The tragedy becomes the triumph. The love story becomes the warning.

Facts are fixed. Meaning is fluid.

***

The writer finished her book. It was popular — won awards, changed lives, all that. But it wasn't my story. It was her story. Her interpretation. Her meaning.

I read it once, and I never read it again.

Because I knew the truth she didn't: there is no single story. There are only perspectives. And everyone who lived through what I lived through has a different version.

***

Now I ask the question: who tells your story?

Is it you? Or is it someone else — a parent, a teacher, a boss, a lover, an enemy? Someone who shaped your narrative to fit their purposes?

The first step to freedom is claiming your own voice. Telling your own story. Deciding for yourself what it means.

Because here's the secret: the story isn't over until you say it's over. And even then...

Someone else will keep telling it.

The question is: will you let them?
