Learns to Fly

1,400 words · 5 min read

Mikey had always dreamed of flying.

He would watch birds for hours, marveling at how easily they soared through the sky. Their wings moved so gracefully, like magic. He dreamed of what it would feel like to float above the clouds, free as a bird.

But Mikey was just a boy. Boys can't fly. Everyone told him that.

***

One summer day, Mikey met an old woman in the park. She had a weird look in her eyes, like she knew secrets that nobody else knew.

"You want to fly," she said. It wasn't a question.

"How did you know?" Mikey asked, surprised.

"I can see it in your eyes. The dream. The wish." She smiled. "I used to want to fly too, when I was your age."

"But how? How did you do it?"

The old woman knelt down to his level. "The first step is believing it's possible. The second step is trying. The third step is getting back up when you fall."

***

Mikey didn't understand at first. He built wings out of paper and cardboard. He jumped off the swings. He jumped off the porch. He jumped off anything he could find.

Every time, he fell.

But he kept getting back up. Just like the old woman said.

***

Then one day, something strange happened. Mikey was running as fast as he could, and suddenly he felt lighter. His feet left the ground — just for a moment, just for a second.

He couldn't really fly. Not like a bird. But he felt what it might be like. And that was enough to keep him trying.

Because the old woman had taught him something important: the dream is just the beginning. The trying is what makes it real.

***

Years later, Mikey became a pilot. He flew real airplanes through real clouds. And every time he looked out the window, he remembered that summer day in the park — when he was just a boy who believed he could fly.

He never saw the old woman again. But he never forgot her words:

Believe. Try. Get back up.

That's how you learn to fly.
