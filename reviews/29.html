Little Mike: Learns to Fly

by Michael Jr

⭐ 5.0 · 2 reviews eBook

Little Mike dreams of flying. With friends John and May, he meets Pilot Thomas who teaches the wonders of airplanes.

Little Mike: Learns to Fly gives its protagonist a dream — to fly — and then puts him in the company of Pilot Thomas, who becomes the mentor that helps him understand what that means. Michael Jr. has a straightforward approach to storytelling that serves children's fiction well: the lesson comes through action, not lecture. Little Mike doesn't learn about courage by being told it's important — he encounters situations where it is required, and he responds.

The mentor figure of Pilot Thomas is well-drawn for the age group. He is knowledgeable but not preachy, present but not intrusive in the narrative. His teaching comes through conversation and example rather than instruction, which models a style of adult guidance that parents reading aloud can naturally riff on. "You could talk about what you saw at the airport" kinds of moments are built into the structure without being marked as educational. The friendship between Little Mike, John, and May is also a consistent strength across the Little Mike series — these kids are genuinely companions, not satellites orbiting a single protagonist.

For parents, this book opens natural conversations about aspiration and realism — not to crush a dream but to understand it. Flying as a dream for a child is universal enough to feel immediately resonant, and the book's treatment of it doesn't diminish it by making it easy. Little Mike earns his understanding of flight, which is a better message than simply getting what you want. Two Amazon reviews, both five stars, suggest the audience for this installment is smaller than Fun at the Beach but no less satisfied. At bedtime, this one carries the right momentum: toward sleep, through wonder.

Key Takeaways

  • Dreams are worth pursuing
  • Mentors appear in unexpected places
  • Courage means trying even when it's scary
Who would enjoy this:
Children 3-8 fascinated by planes. Parents wanting positive values.
Verdict: An inspiring story about following your dreams.
