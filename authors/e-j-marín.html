About E. J. Marín

Marín approaches historical fiction the way an archaeologist approaches a site: layer by layer, with the understanding that what is absent is as informative as what remains. The highlands of central Mexico — where the Otomí communities have maintained continuous relationship to the same landscapes for centuries — provide the geographic and cultural anchor for a body of work that refuses to flatten indigenous experience into a single dramatic register.

Born in Oaxaca and educated at the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México, Marín spent several years conducting fieldwork in the central highlands before beginning to write fiction. The research shows: every scene in Otomí carries the texture of specific agricultural knowledge, ritual calendar, and land tenure tradition that could only come from extended time in the communities being described. The transition to English-language publishing under the M. A. Hale pen name reflects a deliberate choice to reach bilingual and international audiences, and the Spanish-language Horizonte Rojo demonstrates that the English-language work has not diluted the original voice — if anything, the two registers complement each other, with the English-language work gaining a certain precision from the Spanish-language imagination behind it.

For readers seeking historical fiction that centers Mesoamerican perspectives with genuine specificity — not as backdrop or symbol but as lived reality — Marín is an essential voice. The literary project is ongoing: a planned trilogy traces the arc of colonial disruption through three generations of a single family, with each volume set in a different period and a different landscape but anchored in the same continuous relationship to land that the first book establishes.
