About Elowen Tidebloom

"I write about what we would become if we finally understood how much we depend on each other."

Tidebloom grew up near the coast of the Pacific Northwest, where weather systems move in off the ocean with a frequency and force that make the boundary between human life and ecological life feel thin — not metaphorically but practically. Houses near cliffs, roads that flood, forests that have learned to bend. That daily negotiation with a living landscape is the foundation of everything Tidebloom writes. The Lumengrove setting in Symbiont Bloom is not invented from scratch; it is the Pacific Northwest made stranger, the ecological relationships preserved and amplified. Tidebloom holds a degree in ecology from the University of Washington and spent several years working in environmental restoration before turning to writing full time — a transition motivated by the sense that stories could carry ecological thinking further than reports ever could.

The Symbiont Bloom series is Tidebloom's first major literary project, and it shows in the best sense: the worldbuilding has the depth that comes from a writer who has spent real time in the environments they describe, and the characters carry the specific kind of resourcefulness that comes from navigating complex systems rather than simple ones. For readers interested in science fiction that takes ecological interdependency seriously as a narrative and philosophical principle — not just as a setting detail — Tidebloom is a writer to follow closely.
