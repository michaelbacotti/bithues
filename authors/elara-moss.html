About Elara Moss

Elara Moss writes gentle bedtime fiction for children — stories designed not to rivet but to release. The Quiet Hours and its audiobook companions offer short, lyrical narratives built around the rhythms of evening: the slow darkening of a room, the settling of familiar routines, the transition from the noise of the day to the quiet that sleep requires. Moss's writing for children avoids the twin traps of over-stimulation and over-explanation, and instead trusts the repetition, rhythm, and sensory detail to do the work that an睡前 story needs to do. The audio editions — three separate recordings — suggest an author who thinks carefully about how the spoken word lands differently than the written one, and who uses that awareness to serve the listener rather than the production. For families building evening rituals that children look forward to rather than resist, Elara Moss is a reliable resource.
