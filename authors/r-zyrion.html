About R. Zyrion

R. Zyrion writes hopepunk science fiction — fiction that insists on optimism as a narrative and political choice rather than naivety. Resonance Drift is set in Eden Prime, a bioluminescent world where the community has optimized itself for harmony — and then begins to detect a pulse that defies the weave. Zyrion uses the science fiction setting to ask questions that contemporary fiction mostly avoids: what if a functioning society is not the终点 of history but a beginning? What does it mean to maintain hope in a system that has solved its most obvious problems but not its subtle ones? The prose is lush and sensory without being decorative — every detail of the bioluminescent world serves the thematic argument. For readers burned out on dystopian fiction, Resonance Drift offers something science fiction used to provide routinely: a vision of the future worth building.
