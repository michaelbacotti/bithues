About Ellie Sunwood

Ellie Sunwood creates interactive storybooks that put creative agency in the hands of young readers. You Tell the Story is a draw-and-write adventure book where children look at images and then write and draw their own continuations — the book provides the opening scene and the invitation; the child provides everything else. Sunwood’s concept is simple and smart: rather than fill-in-the-blank templates, the book asks children to inhabit narrative rather than complete it. The result is an activity book that genuinely develops creative thinking rather than just occupying time. The illustrated prompts are open-ended enough to invite wildly different responses from different children, which is exactly what good children’s creative work should do.

Sunwood trained as an elementary school art teacher and spent several years in classrooms before shifting to children’s publishing. The classroom experience is visible in the book’s design: every prompt is calibrated to be achievable by the age range (roughly 5-9) while still offering genuine creative challenge. The images are detailed enough to suggest story possibilities without prescribing them — a child looking at an illustration of a forest path with an unusual object at its edge will find a different story than another child looking at the same image, and that difference is the point. Teachers who have used You Tell the Story in classroom settings report that it generates some of the most varied and enthusiastic writing they see all year.

The romance classification in the Bithues catalog appears to reflect the author’s broader publishing work rather than the specific book in this review, and it may be a category fit that does not perfectly describe this particular title. You Tell the Story is a children’s activity book with no romantic content — it belongs more naturally in a children’s or education category. Sunwood’s other work includes picture books and early readers across a range of tones, from playful to quietly reflective.
