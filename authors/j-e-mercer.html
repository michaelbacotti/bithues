About J. E. Mercer

J. E. Mercer writes nonfiction that takes on the largest questions in contemporary style: life, consciousness, the universe, and what meaning is possible within them. The Perfection Cycle is currently available free in the Kindle Store and approaches its subject through the lens of cycles — natural, cosmic, personal — and argues that the human drive toward perfection is itself a cycical rather than linear process. Mercer writes with a philosophical directness that does not require prior familiarity with academic philosophy, yet treats the reader as capable of following a real argument. The free price point reflects an author more interested in readers than revenue — which is its own kind of statement about what writing is for.

The Perfection Cycle draws on cosmology, evolutionary biology, and continental philosophy to build an argument that the universe itself moves through cycles of perfecting and dissolving — that perfection is not a destination but a recurring condition, and that human beings have always been part of that larger pattern. Mercer’s approach is syncretic in the best sense: the ideas are drawn from many fields but unified by a coherent authorial voice rather than presented as a survey. The reader finishes the book feeling that they have encountered a unified vision of reality rather than a summary of other people’s ideas.

The free availability of the book has generated a wide readership among readers who might not have encountered it at a paid price point — the philosophical audience and the self-help audience overlap less than they should, and Mercer’s book sits in the gap between them in a way that rewards readers from either camp. For readers who have found most self-help books too simplistic and most philosophy books too abstract, The Perfection Cycle offers a middle path that does not compromise on either depth or accessibility.
