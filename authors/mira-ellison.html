Author

Mira Ellison

Mira Ellison writes speculative fiction at the intersection of contact linguistics and first-contact protocol. Drawing on a background in comparative literature and SETI-adjacent research, her work explores what it might actually mean to hear from—and talk back to—another civilization. She lives in the Pacific Northwest and is currently working on a series examining communication beyond the binary of language.

About Mira Ellison

Mira Ellison writes first contact science fiction in the epistolary tradition — fiction that unfolds through documents, records, and accumulated evidence rather than linear narrative. First Contact Diary presents the record of a first contact event through the accumulation of first-hand accounts, official documentation, and cross-referenced testimony — creating a picture that is more mosaic than narrative, more evidentiary than dramatic. Ellison's approach inverts the typical first contact story structure: rather than building toward revelation, the diary format means the reader starts with the full weight of the event and works backward, assembling what happened from the fragments different witnesses provide. For readers who want science fiction that treats evidence seriously rather than as an obstacle to plot resolution, Mira Ellison is a distinctive new voice.
