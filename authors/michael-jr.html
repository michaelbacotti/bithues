About Michael Jr.

"Children don't need stories simplified — they need stories that respect their intelligence. That's what I try to write."

Michael Jr. began writing the Little Mike series after noticing a gap in early reader fiction: most books for four-to-eight-year-olds either treated their readers as too simple or leaned too heavily on formula. The Little Mike character emerged from a conviction that young readers deserve protagonists who are genuinely curious, genuinely capable, and genuinely themselves — not precocious by adult measure, but real in the way children recognize as real. The first book in the series, Little Mike: Fun at the Beach, was written during a summer when Michael Jr. was spending time with his own child at the shore, watching how a child's experience of the beach — the weight of salt air, the scale of waves relative to their own body, the particular excitement of finding something in the sand — was both entirely universal and entirely specific to them. That balance between the specific and the universal is what the series has held onto across three books and shows no sign of losing.

The series continues to grow beyond the initial three books, with new adventures planned for the characters that readers have come to know. Each book follows a similar structural principle: Little Mike encounters something that feels enormous and insurmountable, and by the end of the story has discovered that the solution was inside them the whole time — not through magic or adult intervention, but through the kind of imaginative problem-solving that children are naturally good at when they are given stories that trust them to be. Little Mike: Learns to Fly is perhaps the series' most achieved entry — an adventure that earns its ending without cutting corners, and that demonstrates the author's ability to sustain a narrative arc across a very short text. Little Mike: Builds a Robot extends the series' interest in engineering and making into territory that parents have found useful as a bridge to actual building projects with their children.

The Little Mike series is available at littlemikebooks.com, and the author maintains an active presence at @LittleMikeReads on social media, where new book announcements, behind-the-scenes writing notes, and reader artwork are regularly shared. The books are used in early literacy programs and classroom reading circles across several states, a reach that began through word of mouth among librarians and reading specialists who recognized the series' unusual quality.
