# Exabytes Farewell Speech — Draft

*For Coach Bacotti to deliver honoring the 2025–2026 Exabytes season*

---

Good evening everyone.

I'd like to take a moment to recognize something extraordinary that happened right here, in this room, over the past several months.

**The Exabytes — Team 603E** — just finished the most successful season in their history.

This wasn't by accident. This wasn't luck.

Let me tell you what this team did.

They started building in the spring — earlier than any season before. They kept going through the summer, taking notes, brainstorming, iterating. When other teams were just thinking about their robot, the Exabytes had a working robot on the field.

And then — they didn't stop. They kept modifying it. Kept improving it. Kept finding ways to make it better, competition after competition.

At every single event, the other teams looked at the Exabytes robot and said the same thing:

**"Wow."**

Even the judges and referees were cheering for them. Not because they had to. Not because it was their job. Because what this team built was genuinely impressive — innovative, original, and unlike anything else at the competition.

Their engineering notebook was the best anyone had ever seen.

And when the scores came in, the results matched the effort:

**They won an award at every single competition they entered.**

That's not a fluke. That's a pattern. That's a team that showed up every single time, prepared and ready, and delivered.

But I want them to know: **what they built here matters.**

They built something that made other teams stop and stare. They built something the judges remembered. They built something they can be genuinely proud of — not because they won, but because of how they won: early, consistently, and with originality that nobody else could replicate.

So tonight, let's recognize the Exabytes.

Not just for the awards on the table — but for the work that made those awards possible.

Thank you, boys. It's been an honor to watch you build.
