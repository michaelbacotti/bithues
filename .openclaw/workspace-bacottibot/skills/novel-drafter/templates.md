# Genre Templates

## Romance
- Heat levels: Sweet → Sensual → Erotic
- Trope examples: enemies-to-lovers, second chance, forbidden love, friends-to-lovers
- Beat structure: Meet → Attraction → Obstacle → Dark moment → Resolution
- Word count: 70,000-90,000

## Sci-Fi
- Subgenres: Space opera, hard sci-fi, dystopian, cyberpunk
- World-building: Show, don't explain
- Tech: Ground in real physics where possible
- Word count: 80,000-120,000

## Fantasy
- Subgenres: Epic, urban, portal, romantic
- Magic systems: Consistent rules
- Maps: Helpful but not required
- Word count: 90,000-150,000

## Thriller
- Pacing: Short chapters, quick scenes
- Tension: Raise stakes with each chapter
- Villain: Needs clear motivation
- Word count: 70,000-90,000

## Mystery
- Clues: Fair play (reader can solve)
- Red herrings: 1-2
- Resolution: All threads tied
- Word count: 70,000-85,000

## Historical
- Research: Accuracy matters
- Voice: Period-appropriate but readable
- Setting: Transport reader
- Word count: 80,000-110,000

## Horror
- Atmosphere: Build slowly
- Fear: Unknown > known
- Violence: Implied > explicit
- Word count: 70,000-90,000

## Literary
- Prose: Elevated language
- Themes: Explore complex ideas
- Character: Deep interiority
- Word count: 80,000-100,000

## Children's/Middle Grade
- Age appropriate: 500-1000 words per chapter
- Morals: Subtle, not preachy
- Vocabulary: Age-appropriate
- Word count: 40,000-70,000

## YA
- Protagonist: 14-18 years old
- Stakes: Personal + larger world
- Voice: Authentic teen perspective
- Word count: 60,000-90,000
