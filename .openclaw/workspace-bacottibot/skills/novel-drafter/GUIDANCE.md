# Writing Voice Guide

## Reading Levels

### Adult Commercial
- Clear, vivid prose
- Moderate complexity
- Strong pacing

### YA 14+
- Accessible but not childish
- Some slang okay
- Strong interiority
- Age-appropriate content

### Middle-Grade 9-12
- Simpler syntax
- Concrete language
- Clear motivations
- Shorter chapters

### Chapter Book 7-9
- Very short sentences
- Simple vocabulary
- Explicit emotions

---

## POV Options
- Third person limited (most versatile)
- First person (intimate, unreliable narrator)
- Multiple POV (epic scope)

## Tense
- Past tense (standard)
- Present tense (suspense/action)

## Scene Construction
1. Goal - What does POV character want?
2. Opposition - What's in their way?
3. Change - What changes (stakes, knowledge, emotion)?

## Dialogue
- Subtext: Characters don't say exactly what they mean
- Distinct voices: Each character sounds different
- Sparse: Less is more

## Description
- Sensory: Engage 2+ senses
- Specific: "Chevy Nova" not "car"
- Active: "The door slammed" not "was slammed"

## Common Issues to Avoid
- Info dumps: Spread across scenes
- Tell vs show: "She was angry" → She threw the cup
- Adverb overload: Use sparingly
- Head-hopping: Stay in one POV

## Chapter Structure
1. Hook (first 50 words) - Grab attention
2. Setup - Establish scene
3. Confrontation - Conflict
4. Resolution - Leave thread dangling

## Genre Heat Levels
- Sweet: Fade to black
- Sensual: Detailed emotion
- Erotic: Explicit (know audience)

## Word Count Targets
- Novella: 20,000-50,000
- Novel: 70,000-100,000
- Epic: 100,000+

## Premium Polish Checklist
- [ ] Vary sentence length
- [ ] Cut 5-15% word count
- [ ] Remove clichés
- [ ] Strengthen chapter hooks
- [ ] Add sensory details
- [ ] Deepen emotional beats
- [ ] Ensure genre expectations met
- [ ] Continuity check
