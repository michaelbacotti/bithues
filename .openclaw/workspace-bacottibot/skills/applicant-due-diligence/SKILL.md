# Applicant Due Diligence

Research workflow for rental assistance applicants to HOUSE Inc. Every new applicant should be run through this process before any money is committed.

## When to Use

This skill triggers when:
- A new rental assistance application or request arrives
- You are asked to verify, vet, or check an applicant
- Someone asks "is this legitimate?" or "is this a scam?"
- HOUSE Inc. is evaluating whether to commit funds

## Core Principle

**Focus on finding SCAM SIGNS, not on judging the applicant's situation.** Legitimate people in financial distress often have minimal online footprint, unusual stories, or limited social media. The goal is fraud detection, not financial auditing.

## ⚠️ Accuracy Rule — CRITICAL

**Report only what you can verify or confirm. Do not invent, infer, or assume facts.**

- Use ONLY what the applicant stated in their own words
- Do not fill gaps with assumptions ("they probably", "likely because", "must have")
- If something is unverified, say so explicitly — do not speculate
- Before drafting any summary or report, ask: *Is this in the record, or did I assume it?*
- An inaccurate due diligence report can harm a legitimate applicant and mislead the board

## Workflow

Run these checks in order. Stop early if a red flag is found.

### Step 1 — Address Verification

- Confirm the address exists and is a legitimate residence (not a business, vacant lot, or PO Box)
- Search apartments.com, apartmentfinder.com, or Zillow for the complex/neighborhood
- Compare listed rent amounts against the stated rent in the application
- Flag if stated rent is significantly higher than market rate for that address

### Step 2 — Landlord Verification

- Confirm the property management company or landlord is a real, registered business
- Check BBB.org, Yelp, and state business registration (NH, ME, VT, etc. as applicable)
- Verify the phone number matches official listings
- Flag if landlord information is vague, missing, or refuses to provide verification

### Step 3 — Name + Contact Database Check

- Search the applicant's name, phone, and email for scam reports
- Check: Google search, BBB scam tracker, FraudWatchers.net, and any known warning lists
- Flag if name/phone/email appears on scam warning lists

### Step 4 — Social Media Presence

- Check LinkedIn and Facebook for corroborating information
- A real person usually has some footprint — but **lack of social media is NOT a red flag on its own**
- Legitimate people in distress often have minimal online presence
- Use social media to corroborate story details (employment, family, timeline) if present

### Step 5 — Story Consistency

- Cross-reference the stated situation against public records
- Check news archives and court records if accessible
- Look for property records that confirm or contradict the applicant's narrative
- Flag inconsistencies in the story told across emails or between the application and reality

### Step 6 — Decision

After all checks, make a recommendation using the report template in `references/report-template.md`.

**The landlord confirmation call is the #1 verification step.** Always recommend it before approving.

After completing research, fill in the report template and return findings to the main agent.

- Lack of online presence alone is NOT a red flag
- HOUSE always pays the landlord directly — any request to send money to the applicant is a red flag
- Always recommend a landlord confirmation call before approving
