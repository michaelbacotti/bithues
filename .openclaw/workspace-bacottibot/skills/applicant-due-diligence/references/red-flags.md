# Red Flags — Detailed Guide

This guide helps identify scam signs and red flags during applicant due diligence. Use this alongside the checklist.

---

## 🚨 Automatic RED Flags (Stop Immediately)

These flags mean **decline the application** without further research:

1. **Name/phone/email appears on scam warning lists** — If found on BBB scam tracker, FraudWatchers, or Google scam results, stop.

2. **Applicant requests payment directly to themselves** — HOUSE Inc. policy is to pay landlords directly. Any request to send funds to the applicant is an automatic scam indicator.

3. **Landlord confirms tenant does not exist** — The landlord says they have no record of the applicant at that address.

4. **Landlord confirms no amount is owed** — The landlord says no rent is past due.

5. **Multiple recent assistance requests** — The same person has applied multiple times in a short period.

6. **Story changes between emails** — The applicant's story materially shifts between communications.

---

## ⚠️ YELLOW Flags (Pause and Investigate Further)

These flags mean **do not approve yet — investigate before proceeding**:

1. **Stated rent is significantly above market rate** — If the applicant claims $2,000/month but similar units are $1,200, something is off.

2. **Landlord is unresponsive or refuses to verify** — A legitimate landlord should be reachable. If they won't confirm the tenant or amount, treat as yellow until confirmed.

3. **Address is a commercial property or doesn't exist** — Verify via Google Maps Street View.

4. **No verifiable business registration for landlord** — A property management company or landlord should be a registered business.

5. **Phone number doesn't match official listings** — If the landlord's number is different from what's on BBB or Yelp, verify before proceeding.

6. **Newly created email address** — If the applicant's email was created very recently (within days/weeks of the application), that's unusual.

7. **Social media profile contradicts story** — e.g., claims job loss but profile shows recent luxury purchases.

8. **Inconsistencies in the application itself** — Dates don't line up, amounts are rounded oddly, story is vague on key details.

---

## 🟡 Watch-Outs (Not Automatic Red Flags)

These are things to note but **do not automatically disqualify** an applicant:

1. **No social media presence** — Many legitimate people in financial distress have minimal online footprint. This alone is not a red flag.

2. **Limited online presence in general** — Not everyone has a LinkedIn profile or Google results.

3. **Unusual or difficult story** — Hardships come in many forms. The goal is fraud detection, not judging whether someone deserves help.

4. **First-time applicant with sparse records** — Someone who has never asked for help before and has thin records is not suspicious by default.

5. **Self-employed or gig workers** — Income verification can be harder for these applicants. This is a documentation gap, not a red flag.

6. **Recently moved to a new address** — Some instability is expected with financial hardship.

## Pattern Recognition

### Common Rental Scam Patterns

**Fake Landlord:**
- Landlord claims to be out of town/country and cannot meet in person
- Requests payment via wire transfer, gift cards, or cryptocurrency
- Rent is suspiciously low for the area
- Email domain is personal (Gmail, Yahoo) rather than business

**Fake Tenant:**
- Applicant uses a fake story and identity
- Usually accompanied by a "landlord" who is in on the scam
- Payment goes to the scammer instead of a real landlord

**Overpayment Scheme:**
- Applicant "accidentally" sends more than requested and asks for difference back
- HOUSE Inc. does not participate in overpayment scenarios

**Clone Listing:**
- Scammers copy real rental listings with stolen photos and lower prices
- Applicant claims to have found an "amazing deal"
- Real landlord has no knowledge of the applicant

### How to Catch Them

- **Cross-reference the landlord's phone number** with BBB, Yelp, and official business listings
- **Ask for a lease or rent statement** and verify with the landlord directly
- **The landlord confirmation call is the single most important verification step**
- **Never send payment to the applicant** — HOUSE Inc. pays landlords directly
