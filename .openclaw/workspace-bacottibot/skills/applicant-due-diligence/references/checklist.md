# Applicant Due Diligence — Full Checklist

Use this checklist for every rental assistance applicant to HOUSE Inc.

---

## Applicant Information (Collect First)

Before beginning research, collect:
- Full legal name
- Phone number
- Email address
- Requested assistance amount
- Stated reason for request
- Address of residence
- Landlord/property management name and contact info
- Current monthly rent

---

## Step 1: Address Verification

- [ ] Address exists (use Google Maps, Street View)
- [ ] Is a residential property (not commercial, PO Box, vacant lot)
- [ ] Search apartments.com / apartmentfinder.com / Zillow for the complex
- [ ] Rent amount from listings matches or is close to stated rent
- [ ] Flag if: stated rent is 20%+ above market rate for that area

**Search patterns:**
- `"123 Main St apartment rent"` — find listed rent for address
- `site:apartments.com "Street Name"` — find unit at complex
- `site:zillow.com "Full Address"` — find rental listing

**Red flags at this stage:**
- Address is a commercial property or vacant lot
- No rental listings found for the address
- Stated rent is significantly above market rate
- Address does not exist

---

## Step 2: Landlord Verification

- [ ] Landlord/property manager is a real, registered business
- [ ] Search BBB.org for the company name
- [ ] Search Yelp for reviews and contact info
- [ ] State business registration check (NH, ME, VT, MA, etc.)
- [ ] Phone number matches official listings
- [ ] Landlord has a verifiable physical address or office

**Search patterns:**
- `"Property Management Company Name" BBB`
- `"Company Name" NH secretary of state business registration`
- `"Company Name" reviews Yelp`

**Red flags at this stage:**
- Landlord is not registered as a business
- Phone number is a personal cell, not a business line, and landlord is a company
- No verifiable business presence (no website, no listings, no registration)
- Landlord refuses to verify tenant or payment details

---

## Step 3: Name + Contact Database Check

- [ ] Search name in Google (full name in quotes)
- [ ] Search phone number in Google
- [ ] Search email in Google
- [ ] Check BBB scam tracker: `bbb.org/scams`
- [ ] Check FraudWatchers: `fraudwatchers.org`
- [ ] Check for any news articles involving the applicant

**Search patterns:**
- `"Full Name" scam`
- `"555-123-4567" scam`
- `"email@example.com" fraud`
- `site:fraudwatchers.org "name or phone"`

**Red flags at this stage:**
- Name appears on multiple scam warning lists
- Phone number is associated with reported fraud
- Email appears in scam databases
- Applicant has been flagged by other assistance organizations

---

## Step 4: Social Media Presence

- [ ] LinkedIn search for the person's name
- [ ] Facebook search for the person's name
- [ ] Cross-reference profile details with application (employment, location, timeline)

**Note:** Lack of social media presence is NOT a red flag. Many legitimate people in financial distress have minimal or no social media footprint.

**Red flags at this stage:**
- Social media profile contradicts stated situation (e.g., claims financial hardship but shows expensive purchases)
- Profile is newly created with no history
- Profile name does not match application name (aliases)

---

## Step 5: Story Consistency

- [ ] Timeline of events is consistent across all communications
- [ ] Stated reason matches available public records (job loss, medical issue, etc.)
- [ ] Check court records PACER or state court search for recent judgments
- [ ] Property records confirm applicant lives at stated address (if owner)
- [ ] No major inconsistencies between application and reality

**Search patterns:**
- `"Applicant Name" court records`
- `"Applicant Name" lawsuit`
- State court search for the person's name

**Red flags at this stage:**
- Story changes between emails
- Stated situation is contradicted by public records
- Applicant has recent judgments or evictions that contradict hardship claims

---

## Step 6: Recommended Action

After completing all checks above, assign a recommendation:

### 🟢 GREEN — Proceed
- All checks pass or are inconclusive (no red flags found)
- Recommend landlord confirmation call as standard procedure

### 🟡 YELLOW — Verify with Landlord Call First
- Minor discrepancies found but nothing definitive
- Some checks inconclusive — landlord call needed to confirm
- Any time there is doubt, the landlord call resolves it

### 🔴 RED — Decline
- Applicant name/phone/email on scam warning lists
- Landlord confirms tenant does not exist or does not owe stated amount
- Clear evidence of fraud or misrepresentation
- Story is materially inconsistent across communications
- Multiple recent assistance requests from the same person

---

## Required Confirmation Before Approval

**Landlord confirmation call is mandatory before any payment is issued.**

HOUSE Inc. policy: All rental assistance payments go directly to the landlord or property manager — never to the applicant.

Any request to send payment directly to the applicant is an automatic RED flag and must be declined.
